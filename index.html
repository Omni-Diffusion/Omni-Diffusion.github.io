Introduction


While recent multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have made impressive strides, they mostly employ a conventional autoregressive architecture as their backbone, leaving significant room for exploring effective and efficient alternatives in architectural design. We introduce Omni-Diffusion, the first any-to-any multimodal language model built entirely on mask-based discrete diffusion models, which unifies understanding and generation across text, speech, and images. Omni-Diffusion employs a unified mask-based discrete diffusion model to directly capture the joint distribution over discrete multimodal tokens. This approach enables support for not only bimodal tasks but also more complex scenarios involving multiple modalities. Our main contribusions are:

  • First Any-to-Any Mask-based Discrete Diffusion Model. Omni-Diffusion is the pioneering any-to-any multimodal language model constructed purely using mask-based discrete diffusion model. It demonstrates robust performance on various tasks involving multiple modalities, illustrating the potential of discrete diffusion model in multimodal intelligence systems.
  • Diffusion-Centric Training and Inference Framework. We develop specialized training and inference techniques tailored to the characteristic of mask-based diffusion models. For training, we implement an attenuated tail-pad masking strategy to facilitate variable-length generation and a three-stage progressive training pipeline for effective multi-modality alignment. For inference, we introduce position penalty to constrain generation order and enhance visual quality, alongside a special token pre-infilling strategy to improve spoken dialogue performance.


Methodology


1. Training

  • Three-Stage Progressive Training Pipeline. We propose a three-stage training pipeline to progressively expand the set of modalities and tasks throughout the training process. This approach ensures stability when training a unified model on data distributions with distinct characteristics. To further improve the model’s ability for visual interaction via spoken instruction, we construct a Speech-Driven Visual Interaction (SDVI) dataset that primarily includes spoken visual question answering and speech-to-image generation data.
  • Attenuated Tail-Pad Masking. To facilitate variable-length generation, we propose an attenuated tail-pad masking strategy. During training, we append a random number of pad tokens to the end of each data sample. The mask ratio of pad tokens is specifically reduced by a pre-defined scaling factor to mitigate overfitting and improve generation quality


2. Inference

  • Visual Generation Quality Improvement. The diffusion model tends to decode mask tokens from the beginning and the end of the sequence towards the center, which leads to identical patterns appearing in the top and bottom regions of the generated image. To address this issue, we propose the position penalty strategy to scale down the logits of the last several tokens by a fixed factor. This strategy discourages the model from decoding the beginning and end of the sequence simultaneously, therefore reducing repetitive patterns and improving visual quality.
  • Speech Generation Quality Improvement. We propose a special token pre-infilling strategy that replaces the token at the first quarter of the initial sequence with a special token [begin-of-speech]. This method guides the model to attend to text content when generating speech response, thereby improving the logic and coherence of the synthesized speech.


Performance

Omni-Diffusion demonstrates comparable performance on various task compared with model-specifical LLMs, and shows significant improvement over the any-to-any baseline models.
